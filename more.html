Basic requirements Yams are mainly grown in tropical and subtropical climates. They do not grow well at temperatures below 22°C (71.6°F) and are killed by frost. The optimum temperature for the growth of yams is between 25°C and 30°C (77–86°F). They grow optimally in well-draining fertile soils with a pH between 5.5 and 6.5 in full sun or part shade. Very wet soils promote tuber rot, therefore they should be avoided. I.e yams should not be planted in very wet soils. Yams are propagated vegetatively from small tubers. Land should be prepared for planting by plowing and harrowing. Tubers should be planted in trenches to a depth of 15 cm (6 inches) allowing at least 30 cm (12 inches) between individual plants and 1.5 meters. The soil is often ridged to aid drainage. (5 ft) between rows. Plants are then staked with a 2–4 m (6.6–13.2 ft) support, to allow them to climb and ensure that all parts of the plant receive adequate sunlight. Yam plants should be mulched after planting to prevent plants from drying out. Yams can be harvested at any time after the leaves have started to yellow. The soil should be carefully dug around the tuber and the the tuber cut from the vine. Harvesting is best carried out on sunny, dry days to prevent tuber rot.
