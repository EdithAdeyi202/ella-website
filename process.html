Introduction to Yam Flour Processing Plant

Yam flour is also called cassava whole flour, which is fine powder, a little yellowish and grayish. Cassava flour is different from yam starch. Yam flour is made of whole Yam root, which keep the natural Yam flavor and nutrition and contains more nutrient content than cassava starch. Yam flour is a very popular food in most African countries. Yam flour is widely used in people’s daily life for making bread and cakes. Now, more and more people in different counties and regions begin to like the food made of Yam flour.

YAM (Dioscorea spp)

The botanical name for yam is Dioscora spp. Yam is root and tuber crop. There are various varieties of yam, some of the popular species in Nigeria are stated below:
Yellow yam (Dioscorea cayensis)
White yam (Dioscorea rotundata)
Bitter yam (Dioscorea dumetorum).
Water yam (Dioscorea alata)

Yam contains vitamins such as vitamin B complex and vitamin C. It is a rich source of water Carbohydrate, Calcium, Phosphorus, Potassium, Manganese, Amino acids such as Phenylalanine and Threomine.

Yam can be processed into yam flour(elubo), Pounded yam flour(pando yam flour), yam fries and so many others

YAM FLOUR

Yam flour, also known as Elubo is used to prepare Amala, which is mostly consumed by the Yoruba people from the south-west of Nigeria. I.e people from places like Ekiti, Lagos, Ogun, Ondo, Osun, Oyo

There are two major varieties of yam used for producing yam flour, they are Ikokore and White yam.

Equipments used in processing yam flour are Slicing Machines, Per-boilers, Dryers, milling machines (Hammermill with cyclone), sifting machines, sealers, packaging machines, bowls, knives, scoops and weighing scales.

FLOW CHART

Below is a flow chat, discribing how yam flour is produced

  • Cleaning: Clean equipments to remove sand and dirts
  • Slicing: Slice already peeled yam tubers into uniform size chips. This is to enable the chips to dry faster at the same time and temperature.
  • Washing: Wash/Rinse chips with clean water
  • Drying: Dry yam chips in a dryer. Ensure yam chips are properly dried before milling.
  • Milling: Mill dried chips into a fine flour using a hammer mill
  • Cooling: Cool milled flour before packaging to reduce the heat generated as a result of milling
  • Sieving: Sieve yam flour to remove particles
  • Packaging: Package yam flour in different sizes (Grams(g) or Kilograms(kg)) seal and label
  • Marketing: Your packaged yam flour can be sold to individuals, supermarkets, schools, restaurants, hotels, and caterers. Yam flour can also be transported to other states and countries.
